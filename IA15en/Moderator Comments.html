Criterion A: Planning

The client is stated without evidence of consultation and the scenario is described. The advisor being the candidate herself is not acceptable – essentially there is no advisor. Some rationale is given for the software but only minimally so for the product. The criteria for success could have been more specific and testable. This is a borderline 3/4 and best fit was applied. This criterion could have been improved with more detail, explicit evidence of client consultation and some more specific criteria for success.

Criterion B: Solution overview

The record of tasks does not include testing or implementing and design is implicit – partially complete. Candidates should be guided to explicitly address all 5 stages (plan, design, develop, test and implement). The design overview has serious omissions and it is difficult to see how the product was developed. A test plan is outlined but does not address all criteria for success.

Criterion C: Development

The idea is complex but the implementation of the solution is only moderately complex. Furthermore, techniques have not been explained properly – moderate to low complexity. There is little evidence of algorithmic thinking (either in B or C) and creativity in problem solving (as evident in the video) – low ingenuity. Candidates should aim to explain their understanding of and the use of the techniques involved and to showcase their algorithmic thinking and creativity.

Criterion D: Functionality and extensibility of product

The video only shows partial functionality (as mentioned by candidate). Extensibility is impacted by the rudimentary design overview. A proper design effort would have given this candidate a better chance at creating a successful solution.

Criterion E: Evaluation

All criteria for success are addressed but there is no explicit evidence of client/adviser feedback. The recommendations for improvement are vague and do not go beyond the unfulfilled criteria for success. A proper evaluation should include a discussion of success criteria based on significant client/adviser feedback and recommendations for improvement should be justified.
