General comments

The code solution developed for Richard Mulkman was developed according to the guidelines for internal assessment in the guide.

The work illustrates that it is not necessary to write inordinate amounts of code to achieve high marks in the internal assessment.

The overall word count for this solution is approximately 1,400; however, some of the explanations are brief and with an additional 100–200 words the solution could have achieved full marks.

To ensure the conversion to .pdf was successful all image files used in the documentation were saved in the documentation folder.

Criterion A: Planning

A suitable scenario has been investigated with a client clearly identified. There is evidence of consultation with the client, both in this criterion and also in criterion B.

The justification for the proposed solution is detailed.

The success criteria stated are realistic and can be easily evaluated in criterion E.

Criterion B: Solution overview

The Record of tasks form is detailed and complete providing clear evidence of consultation with the client that supports the evidence in criterion A.

The designs provide a clear indication of how the product was developed. They also make it easy for the moderator to see how the product could be further developed (extensibility, criterion D).

The range of tests used is appropriate for the product.

Some extended writing is included in this criterion.

Criterion C: Development

The product has been developed to an appropriate level of complexity, and ingenuity has been used to develop customized user interfaces.

The level of understanding is high, but screenshots needed to be used more widely to illustrate the appropriateness of the techniques and how they fit into the overall structure of the code.

The word count for this criterion is only 300, which is far lower than the recommended figures in the "Word count and format" section of this document. The lack of detailed explanation and citing of the source of the map, although it helped to explain the rationale behind the structure of the program, accounted for the loss of marks in this criterion. It was also felt that the complexity and ingenuity of the product and the algorithmic thinking in the development of the product compensated for the omissions and using best-fit a mark of 9 was awarded.

The word count has been determined by the use of all extended text, either written in paragraphs or within tables, where it explains the selection or operation of a particular technique.

The following are not included in the word count:

  • titles and subtitles
  • labels for screenshots
  • text within screenshots
  • bullet points and text within tables (unless they include descriptions or explanations—this text, which is included in the word count, has been highlighted in green for illustrative purposes only)
  • citations
  • code.

Teachers and moderators will be expected to use their professional judgment in determining what is included in the word count and will not be required to examine each piece of text.

Criterion D: Functionality and extensibility of product

The product functions as intended as evidenced from the range of videos as well as using the product on the CD-ROM. However, in some cases different versions of Python will not run, and the video allows the moderator to gain an insight into the product. The additional videos provide more evidence of the product’s functionality.

The level of detail in the product designs in criterion B and the information relating to the development of the product in criterion C provide clear evidence that the product could easily be extended by a third party.

Criterion E: Evaluation

Direct references are made to the success criteria that were stated in criterion A.

The recommendations for improvement are realistic.
