Criterion A: Planning

The client is clearly stated without explicit evidence of consultation. The scenario is clearly described. A rationale for the software is explained and a rationale for the product is mentioned. The criteria for success are specific and testable. To achieve in the highest descriptor, evidence of client consultation should be included in an appendix and it should be referred to explicitly.

Criterion B: Solution overview

The record of tasks includes all stages though implementation is implicit. Candidates should be guided to explicitly address the 5 stages (plan, design, develop, test and implement). The design overview is detailed, but should have been explained for better understanding. A test plan is partially complete because it does not clearly address the criteria for success. For full marks, the design overview should include UML diagrams with interdependencies explained and more detailed algorithm design. The test plan should address all success criteria.

Criterion C: Development

The complexity of the solution is sufficiently high, but most techniques have not been explained well – moderate Complexity. There is some evidence of algorithmic thinking and creativity, but it is not well explained – moderate Ingenuity. Candidates should take this opportunity to explain their understanding and the use of the techniques involved and to showcase their algorithmic thinking and creativity.

Criterion D: Functionality and extensibility of product

The video evidences partial functionality only because it does not address all criteria for success, for example delete is not shown and history of changes is not shown. Extensibility is fairly straightforward, even though the code has not been fully annotated. Best fit is a 3.

Criterion E: Evaluation

All criteria for success are addressed and the recommendations are realistic, but there is no evidence of client/adviser feedback. A proper evaluation should include a discussion of success criteria based on significant client/adviser feedback and recommendations for improvement should be justified.
