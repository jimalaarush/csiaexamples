General comments

The spreadsheet developed for Margaret Smith was developed according to the guidelines for internal assessment in the guide.

The work illustrates the integration of code with a commonly used software tool to provide additional functionality.

The two-stage development of the product is acceptable, but there are issues relating to extensibility (criterion D) as the initial product was developed in a very short timescale to resolve a specific issue. However, this should not dissuade students from adopting this approach.

The overall word count for the work was approximately 1,210 words.

Criterion A: Planning

A suitable scenario has been investigated with a client clearly identified. However, there is some confusion in the relationship between the client and other colleagues and their input into the development of the product. There is evidence of consultation with the client, both in this criterion and also in criterion B.

The justification for the proposed solution is weak, which prevents the work from achieving full marks for this criterion.

The success criteria stated are realistic and can be easily evaluated in criterion E. However, success criterion 10 is unnecessary as the intention was that the product would not require the user to have to access and modify the code.

Criterion B: Solution overview

The Record of tasks form is detailed and complete, providing clear evidence of consultation with the client that supports the evidence in criterion A. The two-stage development is clearly illustrated.

It was necessary to watch the video and open the product prior to studying the design information to get an understanding of the designs. Only at this point did the workbook overview diagram make sense.

The designs are at a range of levels, are extremely detailed and comprehensive, providing a clear indication of how the product was developed. They also make it easy for the moderator to see how the product could be further developed (extensibility, criterion D).

The range of tests used is appropriate for the product.

Criterion C: Development

The product has been developed to an appropriate level of complexity, and ingenuity has been used to develop the customized user interfaces.

The use of screenshots is good, and it is clear from the information how and why the final product was developed. However, the explanation for the key algorithms used is rather brief, but this criterion was awarded 12 marks using best-fit.

The word count for this criterion is approximately 630 words. This has been determined by the use of all extended text, either written in paragraphs or within tables, where it explains the selection or operation of a particular technique.

The following are not included in the word count:

  • titles and subtitles
  • labels for screenshots
  • text within screenshots
  • bullet points and text within tables (unless they include descriptions or explanations)
  • citations
  • code.

Teachers and moderators will be expected to use their professional judgment in determining what is included in the word count and will not be required to examine each piece of text.

Criterion D: Functionality and extensibility of product

The product functions as intended as evidenced from both the video as well as using the product on the CD-ROM (which is not required, but does allow the moderator to gain a greater insight into the product). However, the video could have been improved to show the chart that provides an easily understandable representation of the correlation between the actual and expected scores for question 5.

The lack of detail and clarity in the product designs in criterion B and the use of procedures to make access to the code unnecessary suggest that the further development of the product may not be straightforward. This slightly unstructured development was also evident in the failure to pass parameters.

Criterion E: Evaluation

Direct references are made to the success criteria that were stated in criterion A.

The recommendations for improvement are realistic, but further consultation with client would have provided information to suggest that it would not be possible to export data to the school’s database.
