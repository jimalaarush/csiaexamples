General comments

This version of Mancala was created for the student’s cousin and was developed according to the guidelines for internal assessment in the guide.

The work is of a very high quality and demonstrates examples of how programming based products may be developed.

The overall word count for the work was approximately 1,310 words.

The information about the rules of Mancala could have been located in the appendix if necessary as they do not add value to the work.

Criterion A: Planning

A suitable scenario has been investigated with a client clearly identified. There is evidence of consultation with the client, both in this criterion and also in criterion B. The information about how Mancala is played is brief, but appropriate given the fact that the overall word limit for the solution is only 2,000 words.

The justification for the proposed solution is good, which led to the work achieving full marks for this criterion.

The success criteria stated are realistic and can be easily evaluated in criterion E.

Criterion B: Solution overview

The Record of tasks form is detailed and complete, providing clear evidence of consultation with the client that supports the evidence in criterion A.

The designs are at a range of levels and are comprehensive, providing a clear indication of how the product was developed. They also make it easy for the moderator to see how the product could be further developed (extensibility, criterion D).

The range of tests used is appropriate for the product.

Some extended writing is included in this criterion.

Criterion C: Development

The product has been developed to an appropriate level of complexity, and ingenuity has been used to develop customized user interfaces.

The word count for this criterion is approximately 500 words. This has been determined by the use of all extended text, either written in paragraphs or within tables, where it explains the selection or operation of a particular technique.

The following are not included in the word count:

  • titles and subtitles
  • labels for screenshots
  • text within screenshots
  • bullet points and text within tables (unless they include descriptions or explanations—this text, which is included in the word count, has been highlighted in yellow for illustrative purposes only)
  • citations
  • code.

Teachers and moderators will be expected to use their professional judgment in determining what is included in the word count and will not be required to examine each piece of text.

Criterion D: Functionality and extensibility of product

The product functions as intended as evidenced from both the video and the product on the CD-ROM (which is not required, but does allow the moderator to gain a greater insight into the product).

The level of detail in the product designs in criterion B and the information relating to the development of the product in criterion C provide clear evidence that the product could easily be extended by a third party.

Criterion E: Evaluation

Direct references, including evidence of feedback from the client, are made to the success criteria that were stated in criterion A.

The recommendations for improvement are realistic.
